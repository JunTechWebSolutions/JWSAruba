About SEO Audit for Your Website

In-depth Technical audits

A website audit is the first step in understanding your current website and how it's performing. A website audit is a process of assessing a website on various parameters that contribute to its search engine ranking and user experience. We will scan your entire site and find where we can optimize and make technical changes to improve rankings.

Although you may feel that your website is well optimized an SEO audit could reveal the one critical issue that’s holding your website from doing yet better. Once completed, the SEO audit will provide a definitive ‘To-Do’ list of how to increase your website rankings but please keep in mind that search engines consistently update search algorithms that impact your website placement.

What Type of SEO Audit do I need?

Commonly an SEO audit refers to a ‘technical SEO audit’, however an audit can also be related to effective keyword targeting, content and PR, relevant backlinks, reputation management or negative SEO, or for small local businesses: local SEO. Technical SEO audits focus on the technical aspect of your web infrastructure and often pivots to all other types of audits:

Content Audit: Assesses content quality and relevancy.
Link Audit: Looks at your external incoming backlinks.
Performance: Audit will check page loading and site speed.

We will be looking at the following criteria:

Below is a simplified version of an SEO audit checklist. While every website is different, and there are certainly more that can be included, this checklist provides a good understanding of where to begin.

  • Crawl analysis (errors)
  • Page load performance (speed)
  • Indexation (Accessibility)
  • Mobile performance
  • Site architecture
  • Keyword density
  • Backlinks analysis
  • Image optimization
  • Duplicate content
  • Redirects
  • Robots.txt
  • XML sitemap
